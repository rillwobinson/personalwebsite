My journey into Web Development

I’m currently working with xAlt.fit.

Read my story

I went through the extremely disruptive experience of a severe concussion in the beginning of 2018. Leading up to this I was incredibly active, working full time as a sprinkler fitter, working out five times a week, riding BMX every weekend and working on a podcast. This was all off the table in one quick moment and as time dragged on, I moved into post concussion syndrome. I was feeling a bit better but still unable to handle sound well; even two people speaking at once would cause my brain to stall. Those symptoms combined with nearly constant headaches left me going crazy at home.

Thankfully I have some pretty good friends, and my good homie Chijioke suggested looking into trying to learn web development as a way to pass the time. He had recently finished a bootcamp and already started his new career. I tried  it and things went slowly at first, only being able to concentrate for a minutes at a time before needing to take a breather. But I always went back for more. I had an objective now: to learn something I could apply and use to provide for my family. A much needed respite to the thoughts of ‘what if I never got back to feeling well’?  

After exhausting the surface-level free resources I found on my brief Google searches I purchased an online course and started learning to build websites. Before long I had begun to understand HTML, CSS, Bootstrap and Java Script as well as a some of the back-end bits.
‍
Coming from a professional world of licenses and certifications, it felt foreign to consider myself a web developer, even a junior one, just from some things I learned on the internet. But after a summer of doubting myself and feeling like a poser, I discovered that the secret to get over that was to continue learning and it’s felt like a learning opportunity ever since.

My journey as a web developer has come in waves and continues to surprise me as I look for opportunities to learn and create websites.
